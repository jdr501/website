Welcome to my homepage.

I completed my PhD in Applied Economics at the University of Saskatchewan in 2025. My research takes a macro-finance perspective on crypto market microstructure, focusing on how price discovery, liquidity, and shocks propagate across fragmented trading venues connected through arbitrage and trade routing.

I combine transaction-level blockchain and market data with structural microstructure models to study equilibrium outcomes in decentralized markets. My broader research interests include stablecoins, private money, blockchain infrastructure, market design, financial innovation, and regulation.

Alongside my research, I have taught economics courses at the University of Saskatchewan and St. Thomas More College, ranging from introductory to advanced levels.
